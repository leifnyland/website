About

Hello,

Welcome to my website. My goal here is to give you a sense for who I am, both professionally and personally. I've always loved playing outside, mathematics, and understanding how things work. These passions expanded into outdoor education and a love for computational and physical sciences. I've pursued both since high school, both to the other's detriment.

I attended Appalachian State University to pursue my passions for learning, academia, and computer science. I graduated in five semesters with a Certificate in Data Science and a BS in Computer Science, Departmental Honors. My time at App State focused primarily on computer systems, computer architecture, computational theory, and I fell in love with the mathematics behind computer graphics and deep neural networks. The academia environment is where I thrive; I am most productive, and the happiest, when I am surrounded by peers all focused on learning. I thrive when a problem captivates both my interest and the motivation of my peers, as we can all dive in and love our work purely for the joy of working together on a project we love.

My time at school was surrounded by my desire to focus on outdoor recreation and education. Although it takes away from my focus on STEM topics, I value the health and knowledge I have gained. Before I got my undergraduate degree, I lived and taught skiing in Colorado. I used this time to focus on my physical skill and health, while getting the chance to apply and expand the teaching principles I learned from my 9 shields mentorship in high school. I returned to skiing after college, by fixing up the cheapest van I could find and taking all over North America during the middle of winter. I learned many things during this trip, including impromptu mechanical lessons during the middle of snowstorms. I think it's time to go back to using my brain on computers. I am looking to return to the world of computational sciences, of focusing on mental problems so thouroghly that I lose focus on the rest of the world and just fall into learning, improving, and being pushed on by my peers. I am looking to both step back into a corporate position or return to school to pursue a PhD, whichever path leads me back to learning and growing first.
